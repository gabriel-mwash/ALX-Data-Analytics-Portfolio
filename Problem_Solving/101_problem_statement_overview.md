# The Problem

> **Urban areas in Kenya, especially Nairobi and Mombasa, face severe plastic and solid waste pollution.  
> Streets and rivers are clogged, leading to flooding, environmental degradation, and public health risks.**

In order to understand this problem, we need to ask the right questions to determine whether the problem statement is **concise, clear, and elaborate**.  
The **5W2H approach** guides us in asking structured questions to address every aspect of the problem.

---

## 1️⃣ What is the problem?

> **Plastic and solid waste pollution.**

---

## 2️⃣ Why is it a problem?

> Streets and rivers have been **clogged**, leading to other issues such as **floods**.

---

## 3️⃣ Where does the problem present itself?

> In **Nairobi and Mombasa**, which are major urban areas in Kenya.

---

## 4️⃣ Who is affected by the problem?

> **Residents living in these areas**, especially those close to plastic and solid waste, clogged streets, and polluted rivers.

---

## 5️⃣ When does the problem occur?

> The problem occurs **almost continuously**. As long as there is plastic and solid waste on the streets and in rivers, the issue persists.

---

## 6️⃣ How much of a problem is it?

> Risks include **diseases**, **environmental degradation**, and **clogged rivers** that lead to **floods** in Nairobi and Mombasa.

## 7️⃣ How does the problem manifest?

> The problem manifests as **clogged rivers, environmental degradation, and public health risks**.
